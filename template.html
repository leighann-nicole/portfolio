Writer / Publisher

Leighann Darville, born in The Bahamas in 1996, moved to the Okanagan in 2023 to pursue a career in writing.

Since they were a child, Leighann was interested in books and the words they contained. Given the climate of The Bahamas, they set that dream aside and settled into accounting as a lifelong career with a maybe about writing on the side. Leighann worked over 6 years at Deloitte & Touche before realising that they needed a change. They packed up their home, spouse, and cats and flew to the Okanagan to attend Okanagan College. Since joining the Writing and Publishing Program they have gained a number of skills which are demonstrated across this website.

Leighann is currently unpublished, unemployed, and about to graduate. The future is unknown and full of potential.

Chapbook

During my time as a Writing & Publishing student at Okanagan College I was assigned to design and produce a chapbook of my own creation. Not wanting to pass up what might be a once in a lifetime opportunity, I decided to produce a chapbook of my own poetry. mouthy pt 1 consists of one of three short prose poems written in Bahamian Dialect for submission to a Canadian journal. mouthy was rejected but I was proud of the poems. Given the time constraints of the class I took the first poem sweet tooth for the project. mouthy pt 1 was hand set in 14pt Van Dijck with 48pt Bookman for display. I was responsible for designing, printing, and binding the chapbook. It was printed on a Chandler & Price in The Bunker at Okanagan College. The inspiration for the design was a mimicry of the mouth. The yellow of the title as plague, is an original colour mix made in The Bunker. The cover and flyleaf paper was sourced from what was on hand. By having the flyleaf peek out of the cover, I was hoping to give the impression of a tongue.
